The TapKit Mac app connects your iPhone to the TapKit cloud service, enabling remote control.

## Overview

The Mac app:
- Connects to your iPhone via USB
- Enables Switch Control for gesture execution
- Streams screen content to the cloud
- Executes commands from the TapKit API

## Requirements

- macOS 12 (Monterey) or later
- iPhone running iOS 16 or later
- USB cable (Lightning or USB-C)
- TapKit account
- **Same Wi-Fi network** as your iPhone
- **Bluetooth enabled**
- **Same iCloud account** as your iPhone

## Installation

1. Download the TapKit Mac app from the dashboard
2. Open the DMG and drag to Applications
3. Launch TapKit and sign in with your account
4. Connect your iPhone via USB
5. Trust the Mac on your iPhone when prompted

You need to configure Switch Control in your Mac's System Settings. This sets up the keyboard keys that control Switch Control on your iPhone.

## Connecting to Your iPhone

Once Switch Control is configured, you need to connect your Mac to your iPhone through the Switch Control system menu.

### Opening the Switch Control Menu

You can open the Switch Control menu in two ways:

1. **From TapKit**: Click **Open Menu** in the TapKit Mac app
2. **Manually**: Toggle on Switch Control in System Settings > Accessibility > Switch Control

When the menu opens, you'll see a "Home" switch and a row of buttons:

- Keyboard
- Pointer
- Dock
- Menu Bar
- System
- Custom
- Location
- **Devices**

{/* TODO: Screenshot of Switch Control menu showing the button row */}

### Selecting Your iPhone

Click **Devices** to see Apple devices on your network that are within reach.

Your iPhone will appear in this list. It might be labeled "iPhone" or show your phone's custom name.

{/* TODO: Screenshot of Devices list showing iPhone */}

**How to verify it's the right device:** When you click on your phone in the list, you'll see Switch Control UI appear on your iPhone's screen. That confirms you've selected the correct device.

Click **Connect** to establish the connection.

{/* TODO: Screenshot of Connect button */}

<Note>
We're working on automating this connection process. For now, you'll need to connect manually through the menu. Automatic connection is coming soon.
</Note>

Once connected, Switch Control is active on your iPhone and TapKit can execute gestures.

## Troubleshooting

### Mac and iPhone can't connect

Verify:
- Both devices are on the **same Wi-Fi network**
- **Bluetooth is enabled** on both devices
- Both are signed into the **same iCloud account**
